What is ASL?
American Sign Language — the language of the Deaf community

American Sign Language

ASL is a complete, complex language that uses signs made with the hands, combined with facial expressions and body posture. It is the primary language of many Deaf and hard-of-hearing people in the United States and Canada.

The Deaf & HoH Community

Over 70 million Deaf people around the world use sign languages as their primary means of communication. The Deaf community has its own rich culture, history and traditions. Learning ASL is a gateway to connecting with this community.

Why Learn Sign Language?

Breaks down communication barriers, promotes inclusion, and benefits parents, healthcare workers, and teachers.

Cognitive Benefits

Improves spatial reasoning, memory, multitasking, and creates new neural pathways.
